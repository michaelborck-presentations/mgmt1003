Prompts and Techniques for Strategic AI Use

The Billboard Rule

Before sharing anything with AI, ask: "Would I be comfortable if this appeared on a billboard?" Never share passwords, personal data about others, or confidential information. Always verify AI outputs - remember Deloitte's $440,000 mistake.

Board of Directors Strategy

Get multiple expert perspectives on a business decision in one prompt. Replace [YOUR BUSINESS IDEA] with your actual idea.

Multi-Perspective Analysis

I'm considering [YOUR BUSINESS IDEA]. 
Act as my board of directors and analyse this idea from multiple perspectives:

1. As CFO: What are the financial considerations, risks, and potential ROI?
2. As Marketing Director: How would you position this and reach the target market?
3. As Operations Manager: What are the practical challenges?
4. As Legal Counsel: What regulations or legal issues should I consider?

For each perspective, give me 3-4 key points to consider.

Try These Business Ideas

  • A meal prep delivery service for university students
  • A tutoring app connecting students with recent graduates
  • A sustainable fashion rental service
  • A cashless coffee shop near campus

Devil's Advocate Technique

Force AI to argue against your position to find weaknesses. Replace [YOUR IDEA/POSITION] with what you want to stress-test.

Critical Analysis

I believe that [YOUR IDEA/POSITION].

Play devil's advocate. Give me the strongest possible arguments against this idea. 
Be harsh but fair. I want to know:

1. Why might this fail?
2. What am I probably not thinking about?
3. Who would this NOT work for?
4. What market conditions could kill this?

Don't hold back - I need to hear the hard truths.

Key Phrases That Work

"Be harsh but fair" and "Don't hold back" give AI permission to be genuinely critical instead of polite.

Reverse Prompting

Let AI interview YOU before giving advice. This produces much more tailored recommendations.

AI Interviews You

I want to [YOUR GOAL - e.g., create a marketing plan, write a business proposal, prepare for an interview].

Before giving me any advice, interview me first. Ask me questions one at a time 
to understand:
- My specific situation and context
- My constraints (budget, time, resources)
- My goals and success criteria
- My experience level
- Any other relevant factors

After you understand my situation, then give me tailored recommendations.

Start with your first question.

Use Cases for Students

  • Preparing for job interviews
  • Planning group project presentations
  • Writing cover letters
  • Developing business case studies

Bonus: Assignment Helper

Use AI to improve your own work without having it write for you.

Feedback on Your Draft

I've written a draft for [ASSIGNMENT TYPE]. I want to improve it myself, 
so please don't rewrite it.

Instead, act as a critical but supportive tutor. Review my draft and tell me:

1. What are the 2-3 strongest aspects?
2. What are the 2-3 weakest aspects that need work?
3. Are there any logical gaps or unclear arguments?
4. What questions might my reader have that I haven't addressed?

Explain a Concept

Explain [CONCEPT] to me as if I'm a first-year business student.

1. Start with a simple real-world analogy
2. Then give me the formal definition
3. Show me one practical example of how it works in business
4. Tell me one common misconception about this concept
5. Give me a question I should be able to answer if I understand this

Verification Checklist

Always verify AI outputs before using them. Remember: AI is confident even when wrong.

  • Can I find cited sources independently? (Search for them)
  • Do statistics appear in other reputable sources?
  • Does this make logical sense based on what I know?
  • Would I stake my grade/reputation on this being accurate?
  • Have I read and understood the output (not just copied it)?
